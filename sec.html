SOCIAL ENTREPRENEURSHIP CHALLENGE

Social
Entrepreneurship
Challenge
'17


Social entrepreneurship challenge, SEC was an event organized by entrepreneurship development Cell, BVUCOE Pune. The aim and goal of this event was to solve the underlying and ignored problems of society by using technical and management skills. Our motto was to spread awareness about entrepreneurship and propagate startup culture for the betterment of the society as a whole.
Day 1: - The very first day started with the inauguration ceremony by our faculty head, which was proceeded by his speech. After the speech ‘Rohit Lalwani’, our speaker who has been the guest lecturer for entrepreneurial events at various IITs and IIMs, took the initiative to conduct a workshop. He explained about the Business Modal Canvas (BMC). BMC is a visual chart that describes products’ or firms’ value proposition, infrastructure, customers, and finances. ‘No idea is useless, every idea is worthy of time and research’, was ideated by him. Mr. Lalwani informed us about the obstacles that usually come in the journey of turning an IDEA into REALITY. After the workshop, all the participants were given seven social problems, and on the basis of problems they wanted to work upon, they were divided into teams. Each team had to submit the Business Model Canvas for their idea and pitch the idea before the panel.
Day 2: - Second day of SEC started at 10 A.M. with the welcome speech delivered by the host, which was proceeded by the instructions for the participants. They were supposed to find a solution for the particular problem they chose and form a product or service to solve it, thereby representing it on Business Model Canvas. Participants were given the liberty to work anywhere, but they were to submit the canvas before 5 p.m. Mentors were present, who guided the participants through their idea and also told them about the various ‘do’s and don’ts’. Till 5 p.m., every team submitted their canvas.
Day 3: - Third and the last day was the pitching day. Judging panel was present, before whom each team was required to pitch their idea. Judges discussed every aspect of their product and helped them make it better. The panel comprised of guest lecturers of IIT’s and IIM’s and CEO’s and CTO’s of various companies. Every team was given around five minutes to pitch their product or service through presentation. Since the idea was to solve a social problem through one’s product or service, the judges paid great attention in helping the participants to make their product or service more cost efficient. Every team was rated and ranked according to the efficiency of their Business Model Canvas and in the end, keeping all the aspects in mind, the team which came up with the best product or service and also pitched it in the most effective way, was chosen as the WINNER.
